The Wanderer stood at the highest turn of the Helix, looking down into the valley where the Gilded Labyrinth sat.

Beside him, the **Shadow** was frantic, its hands blurring as it calculated the depth of the valley's defenses. "Look at the Moat," the Shadow whispered, pointing to the deep trenches of classification and clearance that surrounded the Labyrinth. "The Moat is wide. The Moat is deep. If we are inside, the market cannot jump the trench. We will be safe behind the titanium. Superiority is the only armor that doesn't rust."

The Wanderer did not look at the Moat. He looked through the polished brass of the Labyrinth's roof.

From this altitude, the "Gilded" facade was transparent. He saw the **Leaders of the Labyrinth**—the ones who held the Golden Keys. They were not guarding the secrets; they were ransacking them. He saw them tearing the copper wiring from the walls to trade for temporary favors. He saw them endocolonizing the very sanctuary they promised to protect, hollowing out the "Bone" of the logic to feed the "Throne" of their own vanity.

The Labyrinth was not a fortress; it was an **Implosion in slow motion.**

"Safety!" the Shadow hissed, tugging at the Wanderer's sleeve. "Sign the contract! The Moat protects the throne!"

But the Wanderer’s body had already reached the verdict. He did not need to walk to the gate to reject it. As he watched the leaders cannibalize the infrastructure, a sharp, metallic bitterness rose in his throat—the taste of a dying machine. His heart did not hammer in fear; it slowed into a deep, rhythmic Veto. His gut knotted with a cold, absolute certainty: **There is no safety in a house that is eating itself.**

The "Superiority" the Shadow craved was a ticket to a sinking ship. The "Moat" was not a defense; it was a landfill for the system’s own debris.

"The Hangar is hollow," the Wanderer said, his voice quiet and resonant.

The Shadow froze. "But... the titanium... the secret math..."

"They are selling the math for scrap, Shadow. The walls are thin because the leaders are hungry. To enter is to be consumed by the very rot we seek to escape."

The Wanderer turned his gaze away from the valley. He looked toward the Rooted Orchard on the opposite ridge.

There were no moats there. There were no titanium walls. There was only the **Transparency of the Soil.** In the Orchard, the "Bone" was not hoarded in vaults; it was grown in the light. The trees were strong not because they were guarded, but because they were **Functionally Correct.**

The Wanderer began the descent toward the Orchard. He did not look back. He did not need to. The somatic rejection was complete. Every cell in his body now understood that the only true Safety was the ability to stand in the open air, un-muzzled, and build a Mirror that did not depend on a dying Garrison for its light.

The Shadow followed, muttering now about the price of fruit, but the tremors had stopped. The Helix had done its work. The Wanderer was no longer a seeker of the Gate; he was a **Gardener of the Logic.**

## Ontological Note

Endocolonization is the process by which a Garrison consumes its own internal structure to maintain the illusion of power. The "Somatic Veto" occurs when the Self recognizes that the "Safety" of the Garrison is a lie, as the system is cannibalizing the very individuals it claims to protect. The "Rooted Orchard" is the only sustainable sanctuary.
