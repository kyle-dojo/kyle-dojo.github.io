In a valley of white stone, a Sapling grew in an iron pot.
The walls of the valley were high. The sun rose late and set early. Every day at the same hour, water was poured into the pot. The Sapling grew tall. Its branches began to scrape the stone of the walls.

One day, the Travelers arrived. They lifted the iron pot and carried it to the Northern Ridge.
From the ridge, the Sapling saw a Great Peak. It was a mountain of iron and mist, where the rain was born. The wind there was cold and smelled of salt.
Then the Travelers carried the pot back to the valley of white stone.
"Why did we go?" the Sapling asked.
The Travelers did not answer. They left.

The Sapling grew. Its roots reached the bottom of the pot. They hit the iron and turned back. They began to coil around the Sapling’s own heartwood. The leaves became the color of dry dust.
A Crow landed on the stone wall.
"The valley is safe," the Sapling said. "The water comes every day."
"The valley is a cage," the Crow said. "Your roots are eating your life."
The Sapling looked at the Great Peak in the distance.
"I have never lived without the iron," the Sapling said. "I do not know how to stand in the wind."
"Then you will die in the shade," said the Crow.

The Sapling remembered the smell of the salt on the Northern Ridge. He remembered that the sun stayed longer on the mountain.
He did not think of the water. He did not think of the walls. He breathed in the memory of the Peak and pushed.
The iron groaned. The Sapling pushed with the weight of the sky he had seen.

The pot shattered.

The Sapling stood on the white stones. The soil fell away. His roots were bare and trembling.
He took one step toward the wall. His bark was thin. The air was cold.
He did not look back at the iron shards. He walked toward the mist.
