I used to think Japan was the place where my real life would begin.

A distant shore.
A deeper culture.
A chance to finally belong—not out of dependence, but through shared practice and mutual respect.

So I built a plan.

Study. Work. Prepare. Endure.
Then one day—arrive.

But something didn’t sit right.

If Japan was the answer…
why did I still feel the need to think so much before acting?
Why did I hesitate to deepen relationships right in front of me?
Why did “later” feel safer than “now”?

---

One day, a simple realization cut through:

> If nothing in me changes, Japan changes nothing.

In fact, it might make things worse.

Far from family.
Far from familiarity.
Left alone with the same patterns—just louder.

---

So what was Japan, really?

Not a solution.

A symbol.

* Of depth
* Of belonging
* Of earned independence
* Of the life I felt I missed

And symbols are powerful.

They can guide you forward…
or quietly convince you that life hasn’t started yet.

---

Then the question shifted.

Not:

> “How do I get to Japan?”

But:

> “Am I living in a way that would *work* in Japan?”

---

Because what I actually wanted wasn’t geography.

It was:

* Strong bonds
* Shared practice
* Cultural depth
* Interdependence
* A life rooted in meaning, not abstraction

And those aren’t things you unlock with a plane ticket.

They’re things you practice.

---

So I looked around.

Zen practice.
Aikido training.
A senpai I could learn from.
A dojo for reflection and integration.

The structure was already here.

Not perfect. Not ideal.

But real.

---

### First Koan

> If the roots are not growing where you stand…
> what will make them grow somewhere else?

---

I realized something uncomfortable.

It wasn’t that I lacked opportunity.

It was that I was still treating my current life as *temporary*.

Holding back.
Waiting.
Keeping something in reserve for “when it really matters.”

---

But that’s the trap.

The more you hold back now,
the more you arrive empty later.

---

So the path became simpler—and harder.

Not:

* optimize the plan
* think deeper
* prepare more

But:

* show up consistently
* deepen one relationship at a time
* sit still for 30 minutes
* act before certainty arrives

---

### Second Koan

> When you no longer need the door…
> is that when you are finally able to walk through it?

---

Japan didn’t disappear.

But it changed.

From:

* destination
* solution
* future life

To:

* amplifier
* testing ground
* continuation

---

If I can’t live this way here, I won’t live it there.

If I can… then I already am.

---

### Final Koan

> If your life where you stand became deeply rooted, connected, and alive…
> where, exactly, would you need to go?
