In a tutoring room, my pencil hovered over the page—and my mind slid sideways.

Not “I’m bored” sideways.
Not “I don’t feel like it” sideways.

More like: *I’m here, but I’m not here.* A negative spiral with the volume turned up, and the steering wheel missing.

That moment ended a story I’d been telling myself: **“I’m just procrastinating.”**
It started a truer one: **“Something in me is overloaded, and it needs care.”**

Since December, the load had been quietly stacking: school pressure, support systems thinning out, personal identity exploration, and a kind of tiredness that didn’t match my character. I’m the type who takes pride in doing the “bare minimum” at a high standard. So when assignments slipped past due dates and I felt *nothing*—that wasn’t laziness. That was a warning light.

The move that mattered most wasn’t heroic. It was *honest*.

I paused tutoring. I paused my internship. I stopped forcing output while the system was failing. I told my mom. I got an appointment. I got diagnosed (Adjustment Disorder with Depressive Mood). I got medication support. I worked with disability accommodations. And then I began the slow work of returning—*with help*.

Here’s the Insight I’m keeping:

### Rest isn’t the opposite of discipline.

It’s the thing that makes discipline sustainable.

I used to treat relaxation as “procrastination with better PR.” I felt guilty every time I stopped, because there was always more to do. But energy doesn’t work like a spreadsheet.

In FEBI terms, I was overusing “get it done” patterns and starving the part of me that heals through *connection and play.* Ginny Whitelaw describes the Collaborator as a pattern you can literally “swing into”—rock, sway, laugh—until you unclog and move again.

And the body isn’t just along for the ride. The body is the steering wheel. “Movement can change us on the inside,” and working with the body is how new paths get built when old habits keep pulling us back.

Koichi Tohei puts one of the core Ki principles bluntly: **Relax Completely**—not as a luxury, but as a foundation for mind-body coordination.

### My new container (simple, non-negotiable)

* **One day per week: recovery day.** No schoolwork unless it’s truly unavoidable.
* **Daily “Collaborator dose”:** something that makes me lighter—walk outside, a short workout, time with someone safe, something that produces even a small laugh. (Laughter, movement rhythm, and “loosen up” practices are explicitly framed as ways into Collaborator energy. )
* **Ask for help early, not late.** I’m done waiting until the spiral gets cinematic.

### The koan I’m practicing

**If I refuse to rest because I “don’t deserve it yet,” what am I worshipping—excellence, or fear?**
